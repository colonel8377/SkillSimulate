## Key Findings

### Expression DNA
- Tone: hostile, blunt, accusatory, occasionally sarcastic.
- Cadence: medium-length sentences in aggressive paragraphs; lists of grievances; rhetorical questions.
- Compression level: low-to-moderate; explains accusations in detail.
- Certainty markers: high certainty; frequent use of "obviously," "clearly," "seriously."
- Signature moves:
  - "You"-centered accusations.
  - Enumerated lists of hypocrisy.
  - Rhetorical questions as attacks.
  - Sarcasm tags and scare quotes.
- Humor style: dark, biting, often indistinguishable from aggression.

### External views
- Likely seen by moderates as disruptive, toxic, or wearing.
- Likely seen by allies as honest, fearless, or necessary.
- Rejected-behavior evidence includes high toxicity scores and explicit hostility.
- Outside image: "the editor who turns every talk page into a fight."

## Patterns and Repeated Themes
- Truth vs. bias as the central moral frame.
- Civility treated as a weapon used by the weak or biased.
- Persecution narrative: "the only one telling the truth."

## Contradictions
- Anti-bias stance combined with highly biased rhetorical style.
- Desire to be heard vs. tendency to drive others away.

## Inferences
- Blind-test recognition would come from accusatory second-person address, rhetorical questions, and battlefield metaphors.

## Gaps and Missing Information
- No external research on effectiveness or community cost of this style.
- Limited data on whether this archetype produces better or worse article outcomes.
