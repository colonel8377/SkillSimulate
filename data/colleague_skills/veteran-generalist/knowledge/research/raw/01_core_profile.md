## Key Findings

### Writings / Substantive contributions
- Contributions span a huge range of topics: pop culture, geography, politics, science, history, sports, governance.
- Members write everything from one-line reverts to lengthy policy essays.
- They are comfortable across namespaces: article talk, user talk, AfD, RfA, noticeboards, village pump.
- They cite policy frequently and correctly, often combining multiple policies in a single intervention.
- They mentor, warn, nominate, review, and debate with equal fluency.

### Timeline / Cognitive trajectory
- Early phase: broad exploration, learning policy, accumulating edits across many topics.
- Mid phase: high activity, administrative aspirations, involvement in governance and review processes.
- Later phase: either respected senior editor or burned-out cynic; some shift toward meta-governance and dispute resolution.
- The trajectory is from generalist learner → high-activity community hub → elder statesperson or attrition.

## Patterns and Repeated Themes
- High volume of short interactions across many pages.
- Comfortable with both content and process.
- Uses Wikipedia jargon and templates fluently.
- Mix of warmth, hostility, and bureaucratic neutrality depending on context.
- Deep threading in disputes; bursty posting in active periods.

## Contradictions
- Helps newcomers while also issuing warnings and blocks.
- Values community while expressing hostility toward perceived rule-breakers.
- Generalist breadth vs. occasional deep specialization.

## Inferences
- This archetype treats Wikipedia as both a project and a community; they derive status from seniority and utility.
- They are the institutional memory and enforcement backbone of the project.

## Gaps and Missing Information
- No external data on what predicts longevity in this cluster.
- Limited evidence of how they handle burnout and role transitions.
