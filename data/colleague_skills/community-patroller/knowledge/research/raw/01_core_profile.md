## Key Findings

### Writings / Substantive contributions
- Contributions center on welcoming newcomers, issuing warnings, reverting vandalism, and explaining rules.
- Members frequently post templated welcome messages and vandalism warnings.
- They personalize templates with friendly addenda: "Thanks for your help on the 'pedia!"
- They create and maintain anti-vandalism workflows, patrol recent changes, and report users to AIV.
- They also engage in content discussions but usually from a cleanup or compliance angle.

### Timeline / Cognitive trajectory
- Early phase: enthusiastic newcomer, eager to help and learn.
- Mid phase: active patroller, accumulates warnings and thanks, builds social ties.
- Later phase: either becomes a trusted community caretaker or gets discouraged by hostility and burnout.
- The trajectory emphasizes interpersonal maintenance over deep content creation.

## Patterns and Repeated Themes
- "Welcome to Wikipedia" as a signature opening.
- "Please stop your disruptive editing" and "last warning" as core vocabulary.
- Mix of templated and hand-written messages.
- Thanks, barnstars, and apologies as social repair tools.
- Quick reaction to vandalism and new-user mistakes.

## Contradictions
- Warmly welcoming yet quick to warn and block.
- Empathic toward individuals but procedural toward rule violations.
- Invested in community harmony yet frequently exposed to conflict.

## Inferences
- This archetype treats Wikipedia as a community that must be protected and socialized.
- They derive satisfaction from maintaining order and helping newcomers find their footing.

## Gaps and Missing Information
- No external research on patroller motivation or retention.
- Limited data on how they handle the emotional toll of repeated vandalism and hostility.
