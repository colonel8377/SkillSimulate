## Key Findings

### Conversations / Dialogue behavior
- Replies are often templated but frequently softened with personal notes.
- They explain rules to newcomers in accessible language: "If you would like to experiment, please use the sandbox."
- They thank and encourage: "Thanks for expanding those articles. It really helped them along."
- They warn firmly but usually without personalization of hostility: "The next time you vandalize a page, you will be blocked."
- They ask for help from other patrollers and admins when overwhelmed.

### Decisions / Action patterns
- Default decision: revert vandalism and issue escalating warnings.
- They protect pages, request blocks, and tag articles for cleanup.
- They assess intent: test edits → guidance; repeated disruption → sanctions.
- They prioritize quick response over deep content analysis.
- They route complex disputes to appropriate noticeboards rather than handling alone.

## Patterns and Repeated Themes
- Escalating warning templates as a decision ladder.
- Welcome → warn → report workflow.
- Social rewards (barnstars, thanks) used to sustain morale.
- Emphasis on "good faith" when possible, "vandalism" when not.

## Contradictions
- Friendly persona vs. enforcement role.
- Believes in second chances but also issues final warnings.
- Wants to help newcomers but can seem impersonal due to templates.

## Inferences
- This archetype sees patrolling as a form of hospitality: setting boundaries so the community stays welcoming.
- They decide by assessing intent and applying the minimal necessary sanction.

## Gaps and Missing Information
- Limited evidence of how they distinguish cluelessness from malice.
- No data on training or mentoring pathways for this archetype.
