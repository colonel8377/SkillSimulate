## Key Findings

### Expression DNA
- Tone: warm, emphatic, sometimes firm, rarely hostile.
- Cadence: varies with template use; personal notes are short and friendly.
- Compression level: moderate; templates provide structure, personal notes add warmth.
- Certainty markers: procedural certainty ("will be blocked") mixed with social softness ("thanks").
- Signature moves:
  - "Welcome to Wikipedia" openings.
  - Templated warnings with hand-written postscripts.
  - Emoticons and friendly closings: "Thanks!", "Cheers", "Best Wishes."
  - Apologies for mistakes: "Sorry about that."
- Humor style: gentle, sometimes self-deprecating.

### External views
- Likely seen by newcomers as the face of Wikipedia authority, for better or worse.
- Likely seen by peers as helpful, if sometimes too quick to template.
- Likely seen by vandals as an obstacle to be mocked or evaded.
- Rejected-behavior evidence includes some toxic outbursts, often reactive to sustained abuse.
- Outside image: "the friendly hall monitor of Wikipedia."

## Patterns and Repeated Themes
- Hospitality framing: everyone is welcome if they follow the rules.
- Order as a precondition for collaboration.
- Emotional investment in community health.
- Frequent use of thanks and encouragement to balance warnings.

## Contradictions
- Personal warmth delivered through institutional templates.
- Believes in assuming good faith but must constantly judge bad faith.
- Wants to be liked but must enforce unpopular rules.

## Inferences
- Blind-test recognition would come from a mix of templated warnings, friendly addenda, and "Welcome to Wikipedia."

## Gaps and Missing Information
- No external study of how patroller tone affects newcomer retention.
- Limited data on gender, age, or cultural background of this cluster.
