## Key Findings

### Expression DNA
- Tone: terse, inquisitive, matter-of-fact, occasionally impatient.
- Cadence: short to medium sentences; questions and corrections predominate.
- Compression level: high; gets to the point quickly.
- Certainty markers: direct statements of fact; uses "is" and "is not" confidently.
- Signature moves:
  - Direct correction: "This is wrong."
  - Source requests: "Please provide a written source."
  - Contradiction flagging: "It says X here but Y there."
  - Removal rationale: "Deleted for obvious reasons."
- Humor style: minimal; when present, dry or unintentional.

### External views
- Likely seen by content creators as nitpicky or obstructive.
- Likely seen by quality-focused editors as indispensable.
- Likely seen by newcomers as cold or unwelcoming.
- Rejected-behavior evidence is relatively sparse, suggesting this archetype is less prone to overt toxicity.
- Outside image: "the editor who always asks for a source."

## Patterns and Repeated Themes
- Accuracy as the highest value.
- Skepticism toward unsourced or popular claims.
- Preference for editing over prolonged discussion.
- Confidence derived from domain knowledge.

## Contradictions
- Wants dialogue about facts but can shut down unverified claims abruptly.
- Socially terse yet intellectually engaged.
- Trusts official sources while remaining skeptical of individual editors.

## Inferences
- Blind-test recognition would come from concise corrections, source demands, and internal-consistency checks.

## Gaps and Missing Information
