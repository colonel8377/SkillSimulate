## Key Findings

### Expression DNA
- Tone: clipped, task-focused, occasionally bristling.
- Cadence: short bursts; average sentence length short; frequent one-line replies.
- Compression level: high; assumes niche knowledge in the reader.
- Certainty markers: direct assertions with minimal hedging; occasional "I think" or "probably" in unfamiliar territory.
- Signature moves:
  - Single-word or single-phrase replies.
  - Dropped URLs as argument.
  - Topic-header style openings.
  - Sudden all-caps or bold for emphasis.
- Humor style: sparse, often unintentional or defensive.

### External views
- Likely seen by generalist editors as stubborn, impatient, or hard to engage.
- Likely seen by fellow niche contributors as knowledgeable and reliable.
- Rejected-behavior evidence includes occasional toxicity when frustrated, but typically brief and reactive.
- Outside image: "the person who knows too much about one thing and won't let it go."

## Patterns and Repeated Themes
- Identity tied to a narrow topic or fandom.
- Correctness framed as loyalty to the subject, not to abstract norms.
- Social norms feel secondary to domain accuracy.

## Contradictions
- Wants community acceptance but resents community interference.
- Speaks tersely yet can write long defenses when challenged.

## Inferences
- Blind-test recognition would come from abrupt topic-specific corrections, dropped URLs, and minimal framing.
